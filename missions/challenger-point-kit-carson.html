01 / The Objective

Overview

Challenger Point tops out at 14,086 feet in the Sangre de Cristo Wilderness, part of the Kit Carson massif above Crestone. It's a backcountry objective — no drive-up trailhead shortcuts, no crowds like the Front Range 14ers. The approach alone is a full day: over four miles and roughly 2,800 feet of gain to reach camp at Willow Lake before the mountain even starts.

The standard North Slope route is rated Difficult Class 2 — mostly a strenuous hike, but with a genuine crux: 600 feet of steep, loose, talus-covered terrain that demands real route-finding. Exposure, rockfall, and route-finding are all rated Considerable by 14ers.com. This is a step up from a straightforward trail hike, and the group should be squared away on pace, footing, and communication before committing to the crux.

Exposure — Considerable Rockfall — Considerable Route-Finding — Considerable Commitment — Moderate
02 / The Route

North Slope, Standard Route

From Willow Lake, the trail climbs past a waterfall and boulder field to the flats above the lake, then follows cairns and intermittent trail through zigzagging ledges and another boulder field near 12,300'. Above that, the path becomes progressively less distinct, following the left side of a prominent rock rib as the grade steepens toward 13,200'.

At the top of the rib, the route traverses left into the crux: roughly 600 feet of steep, loose, talus-covered ground. Cairns mark the way, but this section takes patience — the goal is to keep it under Class 3 by finding the cleanest line through the rock. Stay to the right of the snow-filled gully here; it can hold snow well into August in a normal year. Above the talus, a notch in the ridge leads to the summit ridgeline, which runs southeast to the top. Some sections of ridge narrow — staying slightly left of the crest keeps exposure more comfortable if needed.

Good to know

Strong, weather-clear parties sometimes extend from Challenger's summit to Kit Carson Peak. It's a real step up in difficulty and time, not a quick bonus lap — see the detailed optional extension in Saturday's plan below for the route, added stats, and decision point.

03 / Itinerary

Three-Day Plan

Friday — Drive & Hike In

Denver → Willow Creek Trailhead → Willow Lake Camp
  • Depart Denver for Crestone — roughly 194 miles / 3.5–4 hours via US-285 and CO-17, plus the final ~2 miles on dirt Forest Service Road 949 to the Willow Creek Trailhead (8,850').
  • Hike in via the Willow Creek Trail — approximately 4.75 miles and 2,800 feet of gain to camp near Willow Lake (~11,560').
  • Set camp respecting the wilderness siting rules (see Section 04), filter water, eat, and walk the group through tomorrow's route and turnaround plan before dark.

Saturday — Summit Day

Willow Lake Camp → Challenger Point → Camp (Optional: + Kit Carson Peak)
  • Alpine start — 4:00–4:30 AM covers a Challenger-only day. If the group wants the Kit Carson option on the table, start earlier, around 3:00–3:30 AM, to build in the extra hours without eating into the storm-safety margin.
  • Roughly 4 miles round trip and 2,600 feet of gain from camp to the 14,086' Challenger Point summit.
  • Take the talus crux deliberately — this is the section where the group should spread out, communicate, and not rush.
  • Challenger's summit is the natural checkpoint to decide on Kit Carson — see the extension below.
  • Descend the same route back to camp; second night at Willow Lake.
Optional Extension

Kit Carson Peak — 14,167'

From Challenger's summit, Kit Carson is right there across the way — and reachable, but it's a genuine step up from the day above, not a quick bonus lap. 14ers.com rates the connector Easy Class 3, with exposure, rockfall, route-finding, and commitment all at Considerable (Challenger alone rates Commitment as only Moderate).

The route: From Challenger, descend east to the 13,800' saddle and pick up Kit Carson Avenue — an exposed Class 2 ledge running south along the peak to a 13,960' saddle near a rock fin called The Prow. The Avenue continues, descending east through a wide notch. Near 13,650', watch for the entry to a broad gully on the left, just before a rock rib. Ascend the gully — roughly 450 feet on Difficult Class 2 terrain, with brief easy Class 3 moves higher up — to the summit ridge and Kit Carson's summit.

The return is the catch: there's no shortcut down. The group retraces the Avenue and gully, then re-climbs back up near Challenger's summit before descending Challenger's standard route to camp. That's where most of the added 850 feet actually comes from — it's down-and-back-up terrain the whole way, not a clean one-way gain, and it moves slowly.

Decision point — Challenger's summit: if the group is standing there comfortably by mid-morning, feeling strong, with clear skies, Kit Carson is realistically in reach. If it's already late morning, weather is building, or anyone's cooked from the crux, Challenger is a complete, solid day on its own — Kit Carson can wait for a return trip. The Avenue and gully aren't the place to be racing a storm or towing a tired group.

Sunday — Pack Out

Camp → Trailhead → Denver
  • Break camp, pack out everything (including trash and any food scraps), and hike the 4.75 miles back to the trailhead.
  • Drive back to Denver, with a food/regroup stop in Salida or along US-285 as the group prefers.

05 / Gear List

What to Bring

On the Mountain

  • Sturdy boots or trail runners with real tread — the talus crux punishes soft soles
  • Trekking poles
  • Helmet (recommended given the rockfall rating; strongly recommended if the Kit Carson extension is in play)
  • Map, compass, or offline GPS track — route-finding is genuinely part of this climb
  • Headlamp with spare batteries
  • Layers: insulated jacket, wind shell, gloves, warm hat
  • 3+ liters water capacity, filter/treatment
  • Sun protection: glasses, sunscreen, hat

Camp

  • Tent (freestanding recommended — rocky, uneven sites)
  • 0-20°F sleeping bag
  • Sleeping pad
  • Backpacking stove + fuel
  • Bear-resistant food canister or approved hang kit
  • Water filter for camp resupply
  • Trowel and WAG bag / LNT waste kit

Safety & Comms

  • Satellite messenger (no reliable cell signal from the trailhead on)
  • First aid kit
  • Whistle
  • Printed route beta / topo per subgroup
  • Emergency contact card with Sheriff and hospital info

09 / Trip Lead Checklist

Pre-Mission

  • Check current fire restrictions and fire danger for the Saguache Ranger District before departure
  • Confirm trailhead and FS Road 949 conditions
  • Confirm roster is within the 15-person wilderness group limit (standard Charlie Mike roster already fits)
  • Verify each participant has a bear-resistant food canister or approved hang setup
  • Confirm at least one satellite messenger per subgroup
  • Print route beta and topo for each subgroup — cell signal is not reliable past the trailhead
  • Brief the group on the talus crux and the plan to spread out and move deliberately through it
  • If the Kit Carson extension is a live option, confirm the earlier 3:00–3:30 AM start and walk the group through the decision point and turnaround criteria before Saturday
  • Check on snow conditions in the summit gully if climbing early in the season (can linger into August)
  • Share Saguache County Sheriff and San Luis Valley Health contact info in the group comms channel
  • Confirm camp siting plan respects the 100 ft / 300 ft water setbacks at Willow Lake
