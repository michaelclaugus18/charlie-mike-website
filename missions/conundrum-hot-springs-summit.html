The Mission

Conundrum Hot Springs sits at 11,200 feet at the head of a long alpine valley outside Aspen. This mission carries the whole group in together, camps together, and takes the whole group up Castle Peak and Conundrum Peak together the next day — off-trail, from the hot springs side — before everyone soaks it off and hikes out together Sunday. One roster, one line, start to finish.

One thing worth knowing going in: because this line goes off-trail above the hot springs, it's a bit more involved than a typical Class 2 walk-up — see the note below.

Good to Know — Two Different Routes Up These Peaks

The standard route up Castle Peak and Conundrum Peak (Class 2, well-traveled, cairned) starts from the Castle Creek Trailhead — a different trailhead, up a different road, 12.5 miles from the Aspen roundabout. It doesn't connect to Conundrum Hot Springs.

Reaching both peaks from the hot springs side — the way this trip does it, so the summit and the soak fit in one continuous weekend — is a somewhat different animal:

  • No maintained trail past the hot springs basin, and cairns are sparse to nonexistent
  • Looser rock underfoot than the standard route
  • Mostly Class 2, with a few short stretches that lean toward Class 3 depending on the exact line taken

None of this is unmanageable for a group with some scrambling experience and a bit of extra care on the loose sections — it just isn't quite the same as a standard cairned Class 2 trail, so it's worth going in with eyes open.

Worth Having

Some prior scrambling experience and comfort finding your own line off-trail will make this a much better day — good to gut-check with new members before they sign up.

Trip Lead

As always, the trip lead is watching weather and pace and will call the shot if conditions turn — same as any Charlie Mike mission above treeline.

Helmets Recommended

Given the loose rock, helmets are a smart call for everyone on summit day.

Weather Window

Afternoon storms build fast above treeline in the Elks, so it's smart to set a turnaround time before heading up and plan to stick to it.

Logistics

Trailhead
Conundrum Creek TH — Aspen roundabout → Castle Creek Rd (5 mi) → Conundrum Rd (1.1 mi)
Parking
~25 spaces, no fee, fills early; never park on Conundrum Rd
Permit
Required — Maroon Bells-Snowmass Wilderness overnight permit, Conundrum Hot Springs Zone, Recreation.gov only
Permit Cost
$10/person/night (May–Oct) + $6 non-refundable reservation fee
Group Cap — Hard Constraint
10 people total in the zone. Whole-group format means the roster itself is capped at 10 — no splitting to manage headcount
Bear Canisters
Required for all overnight campers
Toilets
None — WAG bags required in practice, free at Aspen Ranger District
Campfires
Prohibited at all Conundrum sites, and above 10,800 ft anywhere
Dogs
Not permitted in the Conundrum Hot Springs Zone
Cell Service
None past the trailhead — file a trip plan before departure
Permit timing — verify before booking
Official USFS guidance splits permits into three release blocks: Feb 15, 8am MST for Apr 1–Jul 31, Jun 15, 8am MST for Aug 1–Nov 30, Oct 15, 8am MST for Dec 1–Mar 31. Popular summer weekends have sold out within minutes — permit holder online, logged in, payment ready, at the exact release time.

Gear List

Backpacking — Everyone

  • Backpacking pack, rain cover
  • Tent, sleeping bag, pad
  • Bear canister (rentable — check Aspen outfitters)
  • Water filter + backup treatment
  • Camp shoes / sandals for creek crossing 3 and the soak
  • WAG bag kit
  • Stove + fuel (no fires permitted)
  • Trekking poles
  • Warm layers for after-dark soaking
  • Headlamp, extra batteries
  • Offline map/GPS track — no cell service past the trailhead

Summit Day — Everyone

  • Helmet
  • Sturdy scrambling boots (not trail runners)
  • GPS track of intended line, downloaded in advance — no trail, no reliable cairns
  • Full layering system — no shelter above treeline on this line
  • Stick together — good practice not to split off solo on this terrain

Trip Lead

  • Printed permit + trip leader photo ID
  • Bear spray
  • Weather check morning-of
  • Trip plan filed with someone off-mountain
